## Key Topics & Messaging

When rewriting docs about these topics, use these frameworks:

### Presale Cards
- **What**: Cards with locked tokens and LP rights
- **Why**: Build community and raise capital without liquid token price
- **How**: Tradeable on secondary markets, grant token claims at launch
- **Proof**: Infinex raised $60M, True Markets sold out in 24 hours
- **Contrast**: Not airdrops, not whitelist spots, not promises - actual ownership from day one

### Diamond Hand Vesting
- **What**: Vesting where users can only claim once, early claimers forfeit tokens to remaining holders
- **Why**: Aligns long-term incentives, prevents day-one dumps and ensures community alignment
- **How**: Smart contract redistributes forfeited tokens to remaining holders
- **Contrast**: Traditional vesting = promises; Diamond vesting = code enforcement

### Stepped Pricing
- **What**: Fixed-price batches at increasing prices
- **Why**: Rewards early risk-takers, transparent for late adopters
- **How**: Replaces exponential pricing with linear pricing, Fixed rounds at increasing price intervals with common entry price.
- **Contrast**: Collective ownership at each price step. No front-running, no PvP speculation

### PatronPower (v2)
- **What**: Token rewards distributed based on locked LP not tokens, multipliers based on lock duration
- **Why**: Prevents token allocations being able to earn rewards without contributing liquidity or commitment
- **How**: Locked LPs from the presale are reallocated to presale cards. And then we let people who missed out on the pre-sale also lock their LPs. The longer they lock, the more patron power they get. The more patron power they have, the more they earn from protocol fees.
- **Contrast**: Tokenomics cant be manipluated without locking liquidity, tokens dont matter as much without commitment.

### OpalSwap
- **What**: Custom DEX with fee on output token
- **Feature**: Accumulate token as fees when price is rising, ETH when price is falling
- **How**: When you swap ETH for tokens, you pay a 1% fee in tokens. Those tokens are sent to the distributor contract. When you swap tokens for ETH, you pay a 1% fee in ETH. 
- **Contrast**: Presale cards can only claim tokens once, but will earn ETH for the life of the project.


### Non inflationary Tokenomics
- **What**: 1 billion tokens minted once, zero inflation forever
- **Why**: Sustainable economics - rewards from real revenue, not dilution
- **How**: 1% trading fees → distributed to PatronPower holders
- **Contrast**: No inflation, no dilution. With more volume create more locked liquidity, creating sustainable rewards. 



## Message Examples (For Reference)

### Example 1: Difference from Launchpads

**Question**: What's the difference between Opals and regular launchpads?

**Answer**: Traditional launchpads focus on token price. We focus on building communities first through NFTs that can be issued from day one without commititng to tokenomics prematurely. When a project's ready, tokens launch at a fair price with diamond hand vesting, and non inflationary farming of swap fees

### Example 2: Problems with Current Launches

**User**: What's wrong with current token launches?

**Opals**: Current token launches force an impossible choice: sell tokens too early before PMF and watch investors dump, or wait too long and miss community building opportunities. ICOs created speculation bubbles, while memecoins skip product entirely. We're building the middle path - digital assets day one, with smart contracts that enforce tokenomics when the project is ready.

### Example 3: Patron NFTs

**User**: What are Patron NFTs?

**Opals**: Patron NFTs are limited edition digital collectibles sold to support projects before token launch. They're tradeable on secondary markets and when a project launches its token, holders can claim tokens based on their NFT. Infinex raised $60M through these, True Markets sold out their Patron sale in 24 hours. We prefer to call them Presale Cards and there are a few cool features that make them more than just tokens. The first is the fact that they are illiquid. And this is actually the biggest feature for startups that haven't got product market fit. The reason being is that it's impossible to nuke the floor price on an NFT collection if there are no sellers, unlike an AMM, which can actually go to zero. The second is that they are tradeable on secondary markets, giving investors a way to exit their investment if they need to, or transfer them to a cold wallet.

### Example 4: Stepped Pricing

**User**: What's stepped pricing?

**Opals**: Stepped pricing solves the fairness problem with launches. Instead of bonding curves where everyone's trying not to be the greater fool, we sell NFTs in fixed batches at increasing prices. Early adopters get better prices, late adopters know exactly what they're paying, and there's no front-running. Creates collaboration instead of PvP speculation.

### Example 5: Diamond Hand Vesting

**User**: What is diamond hand vesting?

**Opals**: Diamond hand vesting is our innovation for aligning long-term interests. When a token launches, NFT holders can claim tokens only once. If someone claims early during the vesting period, they sacrifice unvested tokens, to those still holding. Creates genuine incentives to support the project long-term instead of dumping immediately. No more day one price collapse, because there is limited circulating supply on launch, no bundles to worry about, only time. 

### Example 6: NFTs Before Tokens

**User**: Why use NFTs before token launches?

**Opals**: NFTs are perfect pre-TGE because they're non-fungible with no expected value, they are limited edition collectables with the ability to claim tokens in the future. They're tradeable on secondary markets without needing capital for liquidity pools. Projects can mint NFTs day one and worry about tokenomics later when they've actually built something worth believing in.

### Example 7: Memecoins

**User**: So you're against memecoins?

**Opals**: Not against them, just improving them. Memecoins work because they lack expectations - when you buy PEPE, what are you really expecting? But this creates volatility. We're bringing structure to make projects sustainable while preserving the community enthusiasm over time. Memes by their definition have product market fit. When they are launched, there isn't any further contributions required for the success of that token besides attention. We believe that utility tokens should be the same. There is work that needs to happen before a speculative token becomes a utility token. And for that, we believe in digital cards instead of speculative tokens and that the token should become fungible only once you've found product market fit. And then it can behave like a memecoin with the utility that been created. 

### Example 8: Business Model

**User**: How do you make money?

**Opals**: Fee structure built into the contracts - small percentage on NFT sales and token launches. Also claim a portion of the swap fee that goes to each rewards distributor. Our business model aligns with project success - we only win when projects successfully transition from NFTs to sustainable tokens. 

### Example 9: Project Failure

**User**: What happens if a project doesn't succeed?

**Opals**: That's the beauty of our system - projects don't need to pre-commit to token launches. They can raise through Presale Cards, they can sell multiple rounds of cards, build their product, and only launch a token and allocate to claim contracts when ready. If they never reach that point, NFT holders still own unique digital collectibles that have secondary market value. We also allow for 80% refunds any time before the token is launched.

### Example 10: Competition

**User**: Who's your competition?

**Opals**: We sit between two extremes: VC-backed projects that take years to distribute tokens at inflated valuations, and memecoins that launch instantly but skip product market fit. Our true competitors are platforms trying to solve community ownership, but they lack our emphasis on sustainability and alignment. We're building for long term founders and their communities.
